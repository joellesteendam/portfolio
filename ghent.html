Situated on the corner of two busy streets in Ghent, this House is designed with its immediate urban context as a main starting point. The building is neither fully public nor is it a private house. Instead, the building stands in between, with a public ground floor, private apartments and a shared rooftop including a single hotelroom.

The building is designed to change the existing hierarchy of the streets and show the new importance of the bike street. The facade along this bike street is pushed back which creates a new space along the small but sunny sidewalk. To make the public space really for the public, the plinth is designed as an open and inviting place. Natural, warm materials are used in the plinth, and it has large windows which are openable, and people can sit in them on both sides. To make the transition between inside and outside even softer, it optically looks like the material such as the wooden bench continues inside.

The building itself sits quite generic in its environment but gets specific from closeby. It connects with its materiality and horizontality to the rest of the street. The small scale of the neighbouring facades is reflected in the different depths and colours of the façade, which breaks up the large surface of the corner and creates small pieces of façade.
