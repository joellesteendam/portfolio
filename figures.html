In exploring the relation between nature and architecture, the Juergen Teller Photography Studio, designed by 6a architects, was analyzed. Although the gardens appear naturally grown as if they are ruinous or untouched corners of a city, the process of creating the gardens was completely artificial and roughly set. This trend of artificiality or rather ‘staging’ is a theme throughout not only the building itself but also through Juergens works as a photographer. His unconventional, authentic and sensual work is highlighted because of the concrete that serves as a neutral but at the same time richly texturized background for his photo scenes. In this short film the furniture is seen as props of a scenery. As found objects are used to symbolize the interchange between authenticity and stylizing, which aligns to Juergens type of art. The film shows the usage by walking through the building, revealing to be a stage just in the end when zooming out, portraying the contrast between the initial reality and the final illusoriness.
